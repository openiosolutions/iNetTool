Understanding Browser Permissions

Modern web browsers have a powerful permissions system to protect your privacy and security. Websites must ask for your consent before they can access sensitive features like your location, camera, or microphone. This tool helps you see the current status of these permissions for this website.

Permission States

  • Granted: You have given the website permission to use this feature.
  • Denied: You have blocked the website from using this feature.
  • Prompt: The website has not yet asked for permission, or you have dismissed a previous request. It will ask again when it needs to use the feature.

Common Permissions Checked

  • Geolocation: Allows a site to access your physical location, typically via GPS or Wi-Fi data.
  • Camera: Grants access to your device's camera for video calls or taking pictures.
  • Microphone: Grants access to your device's microphone for voice calls or audio recording.
  • Notifications: Allows a site to send you push notifications, even when you're not actively on the page.
  • Clipboard: Lets a site read from or write to your system's clipboard (copy and paste).
