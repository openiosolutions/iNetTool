What is a Browser Fingerprint?

A browser fingerprint is a collection of information about your device and browser that can be used to create a unique identifier for you. Even if you clear your cookies, this fingerprint can remain the same, allowing websites to track your activity across different sessions and even across different websites.

How Does It Work?

Websites can gather dozens of data points that your browser makes available, such as:

  • User Agent: A string that identifies your browser, version, and operating system.
  • Fonts: The list of fonts installed on your system.
  • Plugins: The browser plugins you have installed.
  • Screen Resolution & Color Depth: The size and color capabilities of your screen.
  • Hardware Details: Information like your CPU core count and device memory.
  • Canvas & WebGL Fingerprinting: Advanced techniques that render hidden graphics and text to generate a unique hash based on your specific hardware and driver configuration.

By combining these and other data points, a website can create a "fingerprint" that is highly unique to your device. This tool shows you some of the most common data points used in this process.
