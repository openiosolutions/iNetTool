Understanding Your Wi-Fi Security Scan

Public Wi-Fi networks can be insecure, exposing your data to potential risks. This tool performs several client-side checks to assess the security of your current connection.

What We Check For

  • HTTPS Connection: Checks if your connection to this website is encrypted. An unencrypted (HTTP) connection means your activity on this site could be monitored.
  • Secure DNS (DoH): Determines if your browser is using DNS-over-HTTPS, which encrypts your DNS queries. Unencrypted DNS can reveal which websites you are visiting to your network provider.
  • WebRTC IP Leak: WebRTC is a technology that can sometimes leak your real IP address, even when you are using a VPN. This test checks for such leaks.
  • Captive Portal: Detects if you are behind a "captive portal," which is a login page that often appears on public Wi-Fi networks (e.g., in hotels or airports). These networks can be less secure.
  • Public IP Information: Shows your public IP address and ISP. It also warns you if your IP appears to be from a known proxy or data center, which could indicate a misconfigured VPN.

Based on these checks, the tool provides an overall risk assessment to help you make informed decisions about your online activities, especially on public networks.
