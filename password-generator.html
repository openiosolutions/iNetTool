What Makes a Strong Password?

In today's digital world, a strong password is your first line of defense against unauthorized access to your personal and financial information. But what makes a password strong?

  • Length: Longer passwords are exponentially harder to crack. Aim for at least 12-16 characters.
  • Complexity: A mix of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols creates a much larger set of possibilities for an attacker to guess.
  • Randomness: Avoid using common words, phrases, or personal information (like your birthday or pet's name). Truly random combinations are the most secure.

Why Use a Password Generator?

Humans are not good at creating random passwords. We tend to use patterns that are easy for us to remember, which also makes them easier for computers to guess. A password generator removes this human bias.

  1. Creates True Randomness: This tool generates passwords with no discernible patterns, making them extremely difficult to guess via brute-force attacks.
  2. Saves Time: Instantly create complex passwords without having to think of them yourself.
  3. Improves Security: By using a unique, randomly generated password for each of your online accounts, you minimize the risk that a data breach on one site will compromise your other accounts.

For the best security, use a password manager to store your generated passwords so you don't have to remember them.
